A General-Purpose Algorithm for Hot Pixel and Background Noise Removal for Neuromorphic Space Imaging

Sami Arja*, TBD
Western Sydney University
*s.elarja@westernsydney.edu.au
Report (Coming soon) Code (Coming soon)

TL;DR: How can we filter out hot pixels and background noise while preserving the real signal in sparse environments without prior knowledge about the noise and the signal? How can do this fast and efficiently?



Summary

In this work, we explore various methodologies for filtering hot pixels from sparse event data, introducing a novel evaluation metric specifically designed for assessing hot pixel activity removal. We showcase the efficacy of these algorithms across two applications within the domain of neuromorphic space imaging: star detection and astrometry, and event-based star autofocus. A comparative analysis of existing hot pixel filter algorithms is conducted alongside the introduction of new filtering algorithms. These methods are rigorously evaluated and ranked based on their performance, from least to most effective, providing a comprehensive understanding of their capabilities neuromorphic space imaging.


How does a hot pixel look like?

Hot pixels exhibit linear timeline which can be continuous or discontinuous

Pixels triggered by a signal (i.e. stars) fire less often, but can also have a linear timeline similar to a hot pixel


What cause a hot pixel?

Hot pixels have an abnormally high firing rate of noise events caused by transistor and device mismatch.

They can form a large fraction of the background activity output at fixed positions and they can also be close to a signal.

They have lower threshold values than the assumed value. This means that both positive and negative events are generated

more often than expected.

Source: Low Cost and Latency Event Camera Background Activity Denoising, Guo and Delbruck TPAMI'23

Quantitative analysis: All hot pixel algorithms

How well a hot pixel filter can preserve the signal and how much it can filter background hot pixels?

Input events with labelled stars

Background events (star_gt == 0)

Labelled hot pixels ((star_gt == 0) & (hot_pixels_gt == 1))

Segmenting labelled stars and detected hot pixels


total_signal = sum(star_gt == 1);

FP = sum((star_gt == 1) & (hotpixels_detected == 1))

Signal Preservation Rate = (1 - FP / total_signal) * 100

How well it removes hot pixels from background

TP = sum((star_gt == 0) & (hotpixels_detected == 1) & (hot_pixels_gt == 1))

Background Hot Pixel Removal = (TP / sum(hot_pixels_gt == 1)) * 100

How well it performs in:

Preserving the signal and removing background hot pixels

Hmean = 2 * (SPR * BHPRE) / (SPR + BHPRE)
